About the Summit

Hands-On Readiness Support, Together

The South Florida Personal Narrative Readiness Summit is a facilitator-led, hands-on working event designed to help DBE and ACDBE business owners develop their Personal Narrative, organize supporting information, and improve reevaluation submission readiness through practical, structured guidance.

Unlike a traditional seminar or webinar, this is a working session. Participants will leave with a clearer narrative, organized supporting facts, and greater confidence about their reevaluation submission — not just a better understanding of the process.

GHN Communications brings the same one-on-one strategic guidance methodology used in individualized sessions into a small-group format — maximizing both the depth of support and the breadth of peer learning.

Summit Structure

Working Session 1

Narrative Foundation & Qualifying Event Development

Guided small-group work to identify and structure the qualifying event, clarify the personal impediment, and establish the narrative foundation for your Personal Narrative.

Working Session 2

Economic Impact & Submission Readiness Review

Deep dive into articulating the resulting economic impact, strengthening narrative clarity, and preparing your draft for submission — with direct facilitator feedback.

Who Should Attend

This Summit Is Designed For

📄

Business Owners Without a Draft

If you have not yet begun writing your Personal Narrative, this summit will help you build from the ground up with structured facilitation.

✏️

Business Owners With an Early Draft

If you have a rough draft but feel uncertain about structure, clarity, or completeness, the summit will help you strengthen and organize your narrative.

❓

Owners Unclear on Requirements

If the new DOT Personal Narrative requirements feel confusing or overwhelming, the summit provides practical, plain-language guidance on what is needed and why.

📋

DBE & ACDBE Certified Business Owners

The summit is specifically designed for current DBE and ACDBE certified business owners navigating the reevaluation process under updated DOT requirements.
